# Publication 14: an AI infrastructure company Interview

## Publication Reference

- Title: "an AI infrastructure company's Take: Managing Expectations with Probabilistic Systems"
- Filename: `2025-12-practitioner-interview-2.mdx`
- Key Stats: 70% demo success rate; 30 years of deterministic conditioning; the "doom loop"

## Visual Concept: "The Probability Dice"

A compelling visualization contrasting deterministic thinking (rigid, predictable paths) with probabilistic reality (branching, uncertain outcomes). The left side shows a clean, single-path flowchart representing 30 years of deterministic software thinking. The right side transforms into a constellation of probability distributions and branching possibilities, representing the new AI paradigm.

A stylized conversation bubble frames the transition, suggesting this insight emerged from dialogue. The visual emphasizes the mental shift required: from "it works once, it works forever" to "managed uncertainty."

## Key Visual Elements

- **Left Zone (Deterministic)**: Clean, linear flowchart with single paths, checkmarks, certainty symbols
- **Right Zone (Probabilistic)**: Branching probability trees, dice faces, confidence intervals
- **Transition Point**: Central area where deterministic lines fragment into probabilistic branches
- **70% Indicator**: Prominent probability display (like a gauge or meter at 70%)
- **Doom Loop Reference**: Circular arrow pattern suggesting the iteration trap
- **Dialogue Frame**: Subtle quotation marks or conversation bubble framing the composition

## Color Application

- Deterministic zone: Solid Deep Navy (#1a365d) - stable, traditional
- Probabilistic zone: Gradient Ice Blue (#63b3ed) with varying opacities - uncertain, dynamic
- Transition elements: Warm Orange (#ed8936) - the challenging shift
- 70% indicator: Orange accent on blue background
- Conversation frame: Subtle light stroke in ice blue

## Text Overlay Suggestions

- Tagline: "30 years of certainty. Then AI happened."
- Stat callout: "70% success rate - time to change expectations"
- Alt tagline: "The demo worked. So did luck."

## Composition Guidelines

- Clear left-to-right progression from deterministic to probabilistic
- Central transition creates visual tension and interest
- Doom loop visual in upper corner as secondary element
- Quotation mark design element in opposite corner
- Negative space for potential text overlay on right side

# Publication 15: an AI agent orchestration company Interview

## Publication Reference

- Title: "Inside an AI agent orchestration company: Why 90% of Enterprise AI Agent Pilots Never Convert to Production"
- Filename: `2025-12-practitioner-interview-1.mdx`
- Key Stats: 90% pilot failure rate; 40-50% time on integration; $7 trillion knowledge work sector

## Visual Concept: "The Pilot Graveyard"

A powerful visualization showing a funnel or pipeline where 100 pilot projects enter at the top, but only 10 emerge at the bottom as production deployments. The 90 failed pilots are not lost to technical challenges - they are stopped at business case checkpoints along the way.

The funnel is annotated with blockers: ROI undefined, integration costs underestimated, business case unclear. A conversation element (speech bubble or quote marks) highlights the key insight that this is a commercial problem, not a technical one.

## Key Visual Elements

- **Wide Funnel Top**: 100 pilot icons (simple geometric shapes) entering
- **Narrow Funnel Bottom**: 10 production-ready icons emerging
- **Blocker Checkpoints**: Horizontal lines representing ROI, Integration, Business Case barriers
- **Pilot Casualties**: 90 icons diverted at various stages, fading or falling away
- **40-50% Indicator**: Visual representation showing integration consuming half of effort
- **Dollar Sign Integration**: Subtle financial/ROI symbolism throughout

## Color Application

- Pilot icons: Ice Blue (#63b3ed) at entry
- Production icons: Deep Navy (#1a365d) at exit - transformed, serious
- Blocker barriers: Warm Orange (#ed8936) - the real obstacles
- Failed pilots: Faded gray versions falling away
- Background: Gradient suggesting depth and journey

## Text Overlay Suggestions

- Tagline: "90% fail. Not because the AI doesn't work."
- Stat callout: "40-50% of effort isn't about AI at all"
- Alt tagline: "The ROI question nobody asked"

## Composition Guidelines

- Vertical funnel orientation suggesting flow from top to bottom
- Heavy clustering of failed pilots at first barrier (business case)
- Visual weight increases at bottom where production happens
- Quotation element framing the key statistic
- Clear sense of journey and transformation (or failure)

# Publication 16: Interview

## Publication Reference

- Title: "How Incumbants are Solving AI Agent Identity: The Next SSO Moment"
- Filename: `2025-12-anon-interview.mdx`
- Key Stats: 300+ customer calls; CISO as gatekeeper; MCP identity extensions; 3x coding productivity vs 3-5x cost for other tasks

## Visual Concept: "The Identity Lock"

A visualization centered on the concept of identity as the key to enterprise AI agent deployment. A large, modern lock or key symbol sits at the center, representing the authentication/identity challenge. Around it, AI agents (geometric shapes) are either granted access (proceeding through) or blocked (stopped at the gate).

The visual references the "SSO moment" by showing a before/after: fragmented, inconsistent access on one side transforming to unified, standardized identity access on the other. The MCP protocol extension is suggested through connection standardization patterns.

## Key Visual Elements

- **Central Identity Lock**: Modern, geometric lock or key symbol (not cliche)
- **Blocked Agents**: AI agent shapes stopped at the perimeter
- **Authenticated Agents**: AI agent shapes with "access granted" indicators passing through
- **CISO Gate**: Visual representation of security checkpoint
- **MCP Standard Reference**: Unified connection protocol pattern
- **Before/After Split**: Fragmented access vs unified identity standard

## Color Application

- Identity lock: Deep Navy (#1a365d) - security, trust
- Blocked agents: Faded Ice Blue with orange X marks
- Authenticated agents: Bright Ice Blue (#63b3ed) with checkmarks
- Security checkpoint: Warm Orange (#ed8936) - the critical gate
- Connection standards: Clean white lines on navy background

## Text Overlay Suggestions

- Tagline: "The next SSO moment has arrived"
- Stat callout: "CISOs hold the keys to AI adoption"
- Alt tagline: "No identity? No enterprise deployment."

## Composition Guidelines

- Central lock creates strong focal point
- Agents arranged in approach pattern toward lock
- Clear visual distinction between authenticated and blocked
- Security/identity aesthetic without being ominous
- Speech bubble element containing the "SSO moment" insight

# Publication 18: an AI sales intelligence company Interview

- Title: "an AI sales intelligence company's Approach: Building AI Sales Agents That Actually Work"
- Filename: `2025-12-practitioner-interview-5.mdx`
- Key Stats: 60M data points per company; 600K data points per deal; retrieval as the key; "build blind after step one"

## Visual Concept: "The Data Funnel"

A visualization showing the transformation from massive data chaos to precise, actionable context. At the top, a cloud of 60 million data points (represented as small dots or particles) funnels down through a sophisticated filtering system. At the bottom, only 20 clean, organized data points emerge - the exact context needed for the AI to succeed.

The key insight - "build blind after step one" - is represented by the focus on the retrieval/filtering layer (step one) being the only evaluatable component. Everything after follows naturally if retrieval is right.

## Key Visual Elements

- **Data Cloud (Top)**: Massive, chaotic collection of 60M data points as particles
- **Retrieval Funnel**: Central filtering mechanism - the focus area
- **Step One Highlight**: Prominent evaluation checkpoint at the retrieval layer
- **Emergence (Bottom)**: Clean, organized 20 data points emerging
- **Three-Database Architecture**: Subtle reference to Vector DB, Graph DB, Standard DB
- **"Blind Build" Zone**: Area after step one shown with less detail (intentionally)

## Color Application

- Data cloud: Mixed Ice Blue particles with varying opacity - chaotic but not ugly
- Retrieval funnel: Deep Navy (#1a365d) - the critical component
- Step one checkpoint: Warm Orange (#ed8936) - the evaluation focus
- Emerging data: Bright Ice Blue (#63b3ed) - clean, precise
- Blind build zone: Faded, less saturated - intentionally backgrounded

## Text Overlay Suggestions

- Tagline: "60 million to 20. Retrieval is everything."
- Stat callout: "Build blind after step one"
- Alt tagline: "Context is the moat. Generation follows."

## Composition Guidelines

- Vertical flow from chaotic top to organized bottom
- Retrieval layer prominently featured in middle
- Clear visual transformation as data flows through
- "Step one" checkpoint as focal point
- Speech bubble element highlighting the "build blind" insight
